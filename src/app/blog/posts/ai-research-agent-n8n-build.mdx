## Research Was Consuming My Business

Every client project started the same way: hours of research. Who are the competitors? What are they pricing? What do customers complain about? What does industry news say about this space?

Good research drives good strategy. Bad strategy driven by missing information costs clients money.

But I was spending 3-4 hours per client just on research that an AI could do — if set up correctly.

So I built a research agent. Here's the exact architecture.

---

## What the Agent Does

When I provide a company name or industry, the agent:

1. Searches the web for general information about the company
2. Finds and summarizes recent news (last 90 days)
3. Extracts competitor names and researches top 3
4. Scrapes publicly available pricing and service data
5. Identifies common customer complaints from review platforms
6. Summarizes key market trends in the space
7. Generates a structured research brief in Markdown
8. Sends the brief to my Notion workspace and via WhatsApp

Total time: **8–12 minutes** vs 3–4 hours manually.

## The Workflow — Node by Node

### Node 1: Trigger
Manual trigger (I type in a company name) or webhook trigger from a form where clients submit research requests.

### Node 2: Initial Web Search
Using Serper.dev, search for:
- `[company name] overview`
- `[company name] news 2025`
- `[company name] pricing`
- `[company name] reviews`

Returns top 10 results per search.

### Node 3: Content Extraction
For the top 3-5 URLs from the search results, Firecrawl fetches and cleans the page content. Raw HTML is stripped; only readable text remains.

### Node 4: Competitor Extraction
A GPT-4o call analyzes the extracted content and returns:
- Top 3-5 competitors
- Their positioning
- Any pricing data visible

### Node 5: Competitor Deep Dive (Loop)
For each competitor, the workflow repeats Nodes 2-3 to gather similar data.

### Node 6: Synthesis
A final GPT-4o call receives all gathered data and generates:

```
# Research Brief: [Company Name]
## Overview
[2-paragraph company summary]

## Recent News (Last 90 days)
[Bulleted list of key events]

## Competitor Landscape
[Table: competitor, positioning, price range]

## Customer Sentiment
[Summary of themes from reviews]

## Market Trends
[3-5 key observations]

## Strategic Observations
[AI-generated analysis of opportunities and risks]
```

### Node 7: Output
The brief is:
- Created as a new page in my Notion Research Hub
- Sent as a formatted WhatsApp message with a Notion link

## The Prompt That Powers the Synthesis

The most important part is the final synthesis prompt. Here's the core structure:

```
You are a senior business analyst. You have been given raw research data about [company name] in the [industry] sector.

Your task is to synthesize this data into a structured research brief.

Raw data:
[INSERT ALL GATHERED DATA]

Requirements:
- Be factual. If data is uncertain, say so.
- Use a structured Markdown format with clear sections
- Highlight the 3 most strategically important findings
- Identify key risks and opportunities
- Keep the brief under 800 words

Output the brief now.
```

The prompt constraints — factual, structured, 800 words max — are critical. Without them, GPT produces verbose output that buries the key insights.

## What I Learned Building This

**Retrieval quality matters more than model quality.** A worse model with better research inputs beats a better model with poor inputs. Invest in how you search and extract before optimizing the LLM.

**Loop carefully.** The competitor deep-dive loop can become expensive if you don't cap iterations. Set a max of 3-5 competitors and time out searches that take too long.

**Build in human review.** I always review the brief before sharing with a client. AI research is a starting point, not a final product. The brief cuts my research time by 80% — I do the remaining 20% as critical review.
