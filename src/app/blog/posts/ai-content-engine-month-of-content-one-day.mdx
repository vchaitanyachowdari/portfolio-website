## The Consistency Problem

Every marketer knows that consistent content creation is the foundation of organic growth. Post consistently, provide value, build an audience.

The reality: content creation is time-consuming, creative energy is finite, and "consistent" is hard to maintain alongside all the other priorities of running a business.

The answer isn't working harder. It's building an engine.

---

## What a Content Engine Is

A content engine is a system — partly human, partly AI — that takes raw inputs (your ideas, expertise, experiences) and converts them into a consistent stream of polished content across platforms.

The goal: you provide the perspective and subject matter expertise. AI handles the production, formatting, and distribution logistics.

---

## The 5-Stage Content Engine

### Stage 1: Idea Capture (You, 20 Minutes)

Every Sunday, spend 20 minutes doing a "brain dump" of content ideas. Methods:
- Voice record yourself talking about what you observed, learned, or built this week
- Write bullet points of opinions, frustrations, and insights from the past 7 days
- Review recent client conversations for anonymized insights that'd be useful publicly

This is the only stage that requires your genuine thinking. Everything else is production.

---

### Stage 2: AI Idea Expansion (Automated)

Drop your brain dump into a Make.com workflow:
- AI analyzes the raw ideas
- Generates 15-20 content angles from the raw material
- Categorizes by: educational, opinion, case study, behind-the-scenes, question
- Tags each angle by platform fit (LinkedIn long-form, Twitter thread, Instagram, Blog)

**Output:** A structured content calendar for the week with 20 content concepts.

---

### Stage 3: Production (AI-Assisted, 2-3 Hours)

For each content piece:

**LinkedIn Posts:**
- You select angle
- AI generates a first draft in your voice (based on a style guide created from your past posts)
- You edit: 10-15 minutes per post
- Schedule 3-4 outstanding posts per week

**Blog Articles:**
- AI generates a structured outline with H2/H3 headings
- AI writes a first draft section by section
- You review, add personal examples, refine conclusions: 1-1.5 hours per article
- One polished article per week

**Twitter/X Threads:**
- AI converts a LinkedIn post or blog section into a 7-10 tweet thread format
- You review and edit: 15 minutes
- 2 threads per week

**Instagram Captions:**
- AI repurposes the key insight from a LinkedIn post into a visual-first caption
- You pair with a relevant image or graphic

---

### Stage 4: Visual Production (AI-Assisted, 30 Minutes)

- Use MidJourney or DALL·E to create featured images for blog posts
- Use Canva AI for social media graphics (auto-resize from one design)
- Carousel graphics: AI writes slide text, Canva auto-generates design

---

### Stage 5: Scheduling and Distribution (Automated)

- Content uploaded to Postiz or Buffer in bulk
- Scheduling calendar filled for the week
- Blog post published via CMS
- Newsletter draft auto-generated from the week's blog posts

---

## The Weekly Time Investment (After Setup)

| Stage | Your Time |
|---|---|
| Brain dump | 20 min |
| Review AI-expanded ideas | 15 min |
| LinkedIn post editing (4 posts) | 60 min |
| Blog article (review + polish) | 90 min |
| Thread review (2 threads) | 30 min |
| Visual review and upload | 30 min |
| **Total** | **~4 hours/week** |

For 4 hours of deliberate creative work, you get:
- 4 LinkedIn posts
- 2 Twitter threads
- 1 blog article (2,000 words)
- 4 Instagram posts
- 1 newsletter send

---

## Building the Engine: What It Takes

The engine itself takes 2-3 weeks to set up properly:
- Your style guide (voice, tone, do's and don'ts, example posts)
- n8n or Make.com workflows for idea expansion and content routing
- Platform accounts connected to Postiz/Buffer
- Templates designed in Canva

Once built, it runs week after week.
