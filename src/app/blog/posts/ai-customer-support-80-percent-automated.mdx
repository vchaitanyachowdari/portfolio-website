## The Support Ticket Problem

Every growing business hits the same wall: customer support scale.

When you have 10 customers, you answer everything personally. When you have 1,000, you're drowning. Hiring more support agents works, but it's expensive, slow to ramp, and doesn't scale with sudden demand spikes.

AI-powered support is the structural solution. Not as a replacement for human empathy — but as a force multiplier that lets your team focus on what actually requires a human.

---

## What "80% Automated" Actually Looks Like

Most customer support queries fall into predictable categories:

- "What's the status of my order?" (30-40% of tickets)
- "How do I use [feature]?" (20-25% of tickets)
- "I forgot my password" (10% of tickets)
- "Can I get a refund?" (5-10% of tickets)
- "I have a billing question" (5-10% of tickets)

These are predictable, answerable questions. AI can handle them reliably. The remaining 20% — complex complaints, escalations, nuanced situations — go to humans.

## The Architecture

### Layer 1: Knowledge Base RAG System

Build a RAG system over your support documentation, FAQ, product docs, and past resolved tickets.

**How it works:**
- User submits a support ticket
- Query is semantically searched against your knowledge base
- Most relevant content chunks retrieved
- AI generates a response grounded in your actual documentation

**Result:** Accurate, on-brand answers that reference your real policies and procedures — not hallucinated generic advice.

---

### Layer 2: Dynamic Data Integration

For questions like "Where is my order?" the AI needs live access to your systems.

**Integration examples:**
- Order status: Shopify/WooCommerce API
- Account status: Your CRM or user database
- Subscription: Stripe/Razorpay API

The AI calls the right API, gets the real data, and incorporates it into its response.

---

### Layer 3: Intelligent Routing

Not everything goes through AI. Build a classifier that routes tickets based on:

- **Topic**: Billing, technical, general inquiry, complaint
- **Sentiment**: Angry customers go to humans faster
- **Priority**: Enterprise clients get human-first routing
- **Confidence**: If AI confidence is below 75%, escalate to human

This routing logic is the difference between a good AI support system and one that frustrates customers by mishandling escalations.

---

### Layer 4: Human Handoff

When AI escalates to a human, the handoff includes:
- Full conversation context
- AI's attempted answer (so the agent knows what was tried)
- Customer history from CRM
- Suggested resolution based on similar past tickets

The human sees everything in one view, resolves faster, and the resolution is logged for improving the AI's future responses.
