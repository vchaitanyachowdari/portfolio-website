## Agencies Are Sitting on an Automation Goldmine

A digital agency's biggest enemy isn't bad clients or competition. It's margin compression from operational inefficiency.

The work that agencies charge for — strategy, creativity, results — gets buried under a mountain of operational tasks: reporting, account management communications, content scheduling, brief compilation, invoice follow-up.

AI automation doesn't eliminate the need for great agency work. It eliminates the tax on great agency work that operational overhead creates.

Here are the 5 automations I'd build first for any agency.

---

## Automation 1: Automated Client Reporting

**The pain:** Every week, someone on your team spends 2-3 hours pulling metrics from Google Analytics, Meta Ads Manager, LinkedIn, and wherever else your clients have campaigns — and manually compiling them into a PDF or slide deck.

**The automation:**
- n8n or Make.com pulls data from all ad platforms via API every Monday at 6 AM
- OpenAI generates a natural-language performance summary with highlights and recommendations
- Report formatted as a PDF or Notion page
- Delivered to client via email with your agency branding

**Time saved:** 2-3 hours/client/week  
**For a 10-client agency:** 20-30 hours/week freed up

---

## Automation 2: New Brief → Project Setup

**The pain:** Every time a new project starts, someone manually creates folders in Google Drive, sets up a Notion project page, creates tasks in your PM tool, and sends the onboarding email to the client.

**The automation:**
- Client fills in a new project intake form (Typeform or Google Forms)
- Make.com workflow triggers: Google Drive folders created, Notion page built from template, tasks created in ClickUp/Notion, welcome email sent to client, introductory Slack thread created internally

**Time saved:** 45-60 minutes per new project setup  
**Impact:** Better client first impression, zero dropped onboarding steps

---

## Automation 3: AI-Powered Content Brief Generation

**The pain:** Content strategists spend hours researching a topic before they can write a brief for a copywriter. Keyword research, competitor analysis, outline drafting.

**The automation:**
- Strategist submits a topic and target audience to a form
- n8n searches Serper.dev for top-ranking content on the topic
- AI analyzes top results for structure, headings, key points, and missing angles
- Generates a structured content brief: target keywords, suggested H1/H2 structure, angle recommendation, competitor gaps to target

**Time saved:** 2-3 hours per brief → 20 minutes  
**Quality improvement:** Briefs are more data-driven and consistent

---

## Automation 4: Proposal Drafting From Intake Form

**The pain:** Every proposal starts from scratch or from a messy old template. A senior team member writes it over 2-3 hours.

**The automation:**
- Post-discovery call, fill a structured intake form (client pain, goals, budget, timeline)
- Make.com + OpenAI generates a proposal draft: executive summary, proposed approach, deliverables, timeline, investment
- Output to Google Docs in your proposal template format
- Editable by a team member in 20-30 minutes

**Time saved:** 1.5-2 hours per proposal  
**Quality improvement:** Consistent structure, nothing forgotten

---

## Automation 5: Client Review Request Pipeline

**The pain:** At the end of successful projects, nobody remembers to ask clients for a Google review or a LinkedIn testimonial. It feels awkward and manual.

**The automation:**
- 3 days after project closed as "complete" in your CRM, automation triggers
- AI generates a personalized review request email using project details from CRM
- Sends via Gmail with your usual signature
- If no response in 5 days, sends a lighter follow-up

**Result:** 3x more reviews and testimonials collected, with zero manual effort from your team
