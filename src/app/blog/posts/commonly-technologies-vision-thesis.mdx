## Why We Started Commonly Technologies

There's a gap in the Indian enterprise software market that has always frustrated me.

Large enterprises pay crores for SAP, Oracle, Salesforce. Micro-businesses use free WhatsApp and a shared Excel sheet. But the vast middle — thousands of growing businesses between ₹5 crore and ₹500 crore in revenue — is stuck with software that was designed for someone else and doesn't quite fit.

This mid-market gap is where Commonly Technologies is focused.

---

## Our Thesis

The next generation of enterprise software isn't going to be built the way the last generation was. It's not going to be monolithic, feature-packed platforms that require 6-month implementation timelines and 50-page user manuals.

It's going to be modular, AI-native, and workflow-specific. Software that understands what your business actually does — not generic software that you bend your processes around.

**Three things make this possible right now that weren't possible 3 years ago:**

1. **LLMs can understand business context**: The natural language interface removes the training barrier for non-technical business users
2. **AI can customize without engineers**: Rules, workflows, and configurations that previously required developers can now be set up by AI with natural language instructions
3. **The infrastructure cost has collapsed**: Compute, storage, and ML inference are cheap enough for mid-market pricing

---

## What We're Building

Commonly Technologies is building workflow software with AI embedded at the core — not as a feature, but as the operating layer.

**In practical terms:**
- Software that adapts to your specific process, not the other way around
- Automation that configures itself based on how your team actually works
- Analytics that explains performance in plain English, not dashboards that require interpretation
- Integrations that speak the language of Indian business: GST workflows, Indian banking APIs, vernacular language support

**Our first vertical:** Operations management for service businesses in India — consulting firms, agencies, professional services.

---

## Why India, Why Now

The timing argument is simple:

**Supply side:** The tools to build AI-native software have arrived and are accessible. The engineering talent to build it is abundant in India and getting sharper.

**Demand side:** Indian businesses are becoming AI-ready faster than anyone predicted. The success of UPI has demonstrated that digital-first adoption can happen at extraordinary scale in India with the right product-market fit.

**Competitive window:** The large Western enterprise software incumbents (SAP, Oracle, Salesforce) cannot move quickly enough to serve the Indian mid-market with locally appropriate products. This window of incumbents being too slow is exactly when startups win.

---

## Our Founding Principles

**1. Indian businesses are not smaller versions of Western businesses**  
They have different workflows, different regulations, different team cultures, different software budgets. We build for them specifically.

**2. Complexity is the enemy**  
Every feature we don't add is a choice for simplicity. The best business software is invisible — it does its job and gets out of the way.

**3. AI is infrastructure, not the product**  
Businesses don't want "AI software." They want their operations to run smoothly. AI is how we deliver that — not what we sell.

**4. Build trust before scale**  
Our first 10 customers will know our team personally. We will be obsessed with their success before we think about growing the customer base.
