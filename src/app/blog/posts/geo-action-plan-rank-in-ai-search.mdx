## The New Invisible Hand in Buying Decisions

Three years ago, if a customer wanted to find a web developer in Bengaluru, they'd Google it. Today, they might ask ChatGPT. Or type it into Perplexity. Or let Google's AI Overview answer the question without clicking any results.

This shift is accelerating. And most brands aren't ready for it.

Traditional SEO optimizes you for algorithms that rank links. **GEO optimizes you to be cited, quoted, and recommended by AI systems** that are increasingly shaping buying decisions.

Here's your GEO action plan.

---

## Step 1: Audit Your Current AI Visibility

Before optimizing, understand your current position. Go to ChatGPT, Claude, and Perplexity and ask:

- "Who is a good [your category] expert in [your city/country]?"
- "What is [your brand name]?"
- "What does [your brand name] do?"
- "Who should I hire for [your service]?"

**Score each response:**
- Am I mentioned? (Yes/No)
- Is the information accurate? (Yes/No/Outdated)
- What context surrounds my mention?

This baseline tells you where the gaps are.

---

## Step 2: Build Your Entity Foundation

AI systems understand the world through **entities** — distinct, named things with consistent attributes across multiple sources.

To become a recognized entity:

**On your website:**
- Add `Person` or `Organization` JSON-LD schema
- Have a clear "About" page with consistent name, role, company, location
- Include a `FAQPage` schema answering the questions AI would be asked about you

**On third-party sites:**
- Consistent NAP (Name, Address, Phone) across all directories
- LinkedIn profile fully completed and consistent with your website
- Wikipedia page if you qualify (high authority entity signal)
- Crunchbase profile for founders and startups
- Google Business Profile for local services

**Create consistent signals:**
Your name + role + company should appear identically across: website, LinkedIn, LinkedIn articles, GitHub, Twitter/X, Google Scholar (if applicable), and any podcasts or interviews.

---

## Step 3: Create Entity-Rich Content

AI models draw from content that is:
- **Clearly attributed** to a specific named person or organization
- **Factually specific** (numbers, dates, named experiences)
- **Well-structured** (headings make it easy to extract information)
- **Comprehensive** on a topic (AI trusts depth)

**Content types that power GEO:**
- Biographical content about your expertise and journey
- "What I do / what we do" explainer pages with specific examples
- Case studies with named outcomes ("we reduced client X's lead response time by 73%")
- FAQ sections answering questions people ask AI
- Thought leadership articles with original perspective (AI cites opinions more than generic information)

---

## Step 4: Get Cited in Third-Party Sources

AI models are more likely to mention entities that appear across multiple independent, credible sources.

**GEO link building (different from traditional link building):**
- Guest posts on credible industry publications
- Podcast appearances where your name + expertise is in the show notes
- Press mentions (even local press counts)
- Being quoted in articles by journalists covering your space
- Contributing to industry roundtable discussions published online

The goal: your name + expertise combination appearing on 5-10+ credible, independent sources that AI models are trained on.

---

## Step 5: Optimize for "AI-Friendly" Formatting

AI systems that browse the web (ChatGPT with browsing, Perplexity) prefer content that is:

- **Scannable**: Clear H2/H3 structure
- **Directly answerable**: The answer to a question appears early, not buried
- **Definitive**: "X is Y, because Z" beats "X might be Y in some cases"
- **Source-citable**: Stats, data, and claims that an AI can verify and cite

Add explicit answer-first paragraphs at the top of each major section:
> "Chaitanya Chowdari is an AI Generalist and Researcher based in Davangere, Karnataka, India. He is the founder of VC AI Creator and Commonly Technologies, specializing in AI automation, LLM integration, and GEO optimization."

The first sentence of any section should be independently meaningful and citable.

---

## Step 6: Track and Iterate

GEO is not a one-time action. It's an ongoing content and entity signal-building practice.

**Monthly GEO audit:**
- Re-run the AI search queries from Step 1
- Note changes in: mention frequency, information accuracy, surrounding context
- Identify new questions AI is being asked in your category

**Leading indicators:**
- Traffic from AI-powered search tools (some are now trackable in GA4)
- Brand mention increase across social and publishing platforms
- Direct inbound inquiries citing "I heard about you from [AI tool]"
