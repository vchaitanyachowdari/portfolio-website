## The AI Vendor Problem

Search "AI automation expert India" and you'll find hundreds of results — freelancers, agencies, consultants — all promising transformative results.

Most of them have:
- Nice websites
- Generic case studies
- Impressive jargon
- No real proof of sustained client outcomes

Hiring the wrong AI vendor is expensive in two ways: the money you pay them, and the time you lose while nothing gets built that works.

Here are the 7 questions that separate genuine expertise from impressive-sounding noise.

---

## Question 1: "Can you show me a live automation you've built that's been running in production for 6+ months?"

**Why it matters:** Anyone can build something that works once. Production systems that run reliably over months require real engineering discipline — error handling, monitoring, edge case management, documentation.

**Red flag answer:** Shows you fresh demos built in the last few days. Talks about what they can build without showing what they have built.

**Green flag answer:** Shows a real client system, explains what problems they've encountered over time and how they fixed them.

---

## Question 2: "What happens when the automation breaks at 11 PM on a Friday?"

**Why it matters:** Automations break. APIs go down. Rate limits get hit. External services change their data format. A vendor who hasn't thought about failure modes hasn't built real systems.

**Red flag answer:** "Our systems are reliable." "It won't break."

**Green flag answer:** "We build error monitoring into every workflow. Critical failures trigger a Slack alert with full error context. Here's our SLA for response times."

---

## Question 3: "What do you NOT automate, and why?"

**Why it matters:** Excellent automation consultants are honest about what AI does poorly. If a vendor tells you AI can handle everything, they're overselling.

**Red flag answer:** "We can automate anything with AI." No acknowledgment of limitations.

**Green flag answer:** Specific examples of tasks they don't automate because the error rate is too high, the exceptions are too varied, or the stakes are too high for AI error.

---

## Question 4: "Walk me through your discovery process. How do you figure out what to automate first?"

**Why it matters:** Building the wrong automation first — even if it's technically excellent — delivers no value. Experienced consultants diagnose before they prescribe.

**Red flag answer:** They propose automations before understanding your business in detail. They have a "standard package" that everyone gets.

**Green flag answer:** Describes a process of mapping existing workflows, scoring by frequency and automation suitability, and identifying the highest-ROI starting point specific to your business.

---

## Question 5: "What tools do you use and why? What are the alternatives you considered?"

**Why it matters:** Real experts have strong opinions based on experience. They can articulate why they chose n8n over Zapier for your use case, or why they recommend ChromaDB over Pinecone for your data volume.

**Red flag answer:** "We use whatever you prefer." Vague tool recommendations without rationale.

**Green flag answer:** Specific tool choices with explained tradeoffs: "For your situation, n8n makes more sense because you'll be processing sensitive client data and self-hosting keeps it on your infrastructure. Make.com would be more visual but lacks self-hosting."

---

## Question 6: "What does handover look like? What can I manage myself after the project?"

**Why it matters:** The goal of AI automation should be to give you control, not create dependency on the vendor. Any vendor who builds systems that only they can maintain has created a different kind of problem.

**Red flag answer:** "We'd need to stay involved for any changes." Proprietary tools that only they can access.

**Green flag answer:** Documentation of all workflows, a training session on how to manage the systems, and a clear list of what they'd manage vs. what you can handle internally.

---

## Question 7: "Can I speak with 2 current clients who've been with you for over 3 months?"

**Why it matters:** Happy clients don't need to be asked. Vendors with strong retention can produce 3 references in 5 minutes. Those who can't have a retention problem.

**Red flag answer:** Hesitation. "They're busy." Offering testimonials instead of direct conversations.

**Green flag answer:** Immediately offers to connect you. References are enthusiastic and can speak to specific outcomes, not just general satisfaction.

---

## Why This Due Diligence Matters

The AI automation market is less than 5 years old in its current form. There are no universal certifications, no established quality standards, no easy way to verify claimed expertise.

The 7 questions above function as your quality filter. A vendor who can answer all 7 clearly and confidently has almost certainly earned the right to your business.
