## The Most Valuable Thing I Can Give You Is Transparency

Most service provider pages tell you what they do and why they're great. Few tell you *how it actually works* — the process, the expectations, the realistic timeline, the things that can go wrong.

I'm going to tell you all of it. Because the best client relationships start with honest expectations.

## Who I Work With

Before the process: let's talk about fit.

**I work best with:**
- Service businesses and agencies with 3-30 employees
- Founders who are curious about AI and ready to invest in it
- Teams that have at least *some* documented processes (or are willing to document them)
- Clients who see automation as a business investment, not just a cost
- Businesses generating ₹20L+ annually (below this, the ROI timeline extends uncomfortably)

**I'm probably not the right fit for:**
- Businesses that want magic — that want AI to replace thinking and strategy
- Pure product companies (my specialty is service business operations)
- Clients who want to build a consumer app and aren't sure what they need
- Anyone who needs enterprise procurement cycles — I work with smaller, faster teams

## The Process: Step by Step

### Step 1: Discovery Call (45 minutes, Free)

We talk about your business. I ask about:
- The biggest time sinks in your current operation
- Your team size and tech comfort level
- Your current tools (CRM, email, project management, etc.)
- What "success" would look like for you

I'm learning whether we're a good fit AND whether your situation is one where automation can create meaningful impact.

**You'll know at the end of this call** whether I think there's a strong ROI case, which specific workflows I'd target first, and what a rough timeline and investment looks like.

No pressure. No forced next steps. Just honest assessment.

---

### Step 2: Workflow Audit (Week 1)

If we decide to work together, the first week is about understanding your business in detail.

I'll ask you and your team to:
- Document your 10 most frequent processes (structured templates provided)
- Walk me through 2-3 of them live (Loom recordings or a 30-minute Zoom)
- Share access to your current tools for me to assess how they're used

**My output:** A prioritized automation opportunity map ranking your processes by impact, automation suitability, and implementation complexity.

You'll see exactly where your time is being lost and what we should build first.

---

### Step 3: Design and Approval (Week 1-2)

For the top 3-5 workflows we're targeting, I'll create:
- A workflow diagram showing how data moves and what decisions are made
- A plain-English description of what each automation does
- An explanation of what happens when things go wrong (error handling)
- The tool recommendations and why

**You approve before I build anything.** No surprises.

---

### Step 4: Build (Week 2-4)

This is where I build your automations in n8n, Make.com, or a combination.

For each automation:
1. Build the skeleton (trigger → actions → output)
2. Add AI layers where relevant (summarization, generation, classification)
3. Add error handling and monitoring
4. Test with real data including edge cases

You don't need to be involved much here. I'll send a daily Loom update on progress.

---

### Step 5: Testing Sprint (Week 4-5)

Before I hand anything over, we test together.

I'll:
- Walk you through each automation in a live session
- Run 10-20 real-world test cases per workflow
- Document what went wrong and how it was fixed
- Show you the monitoring dashboard

You'll deliberately try to break things. I want you to find problems now, not in production.

---

### Step 6: Handover and Training (Week 5-6)

When automations are production-ready:
- Full documentation of every workflow (what it does, how to pause it, what to do if it breaks)
- A 1-hour training session for you and relevant team members
- A written guide on monitoring and basic troubleshooting
- Clear instructions for what to do if something unexpected happens

**You leave with full control of your systems.** Not dependency on me.

---

### Step 7: Ongoing Support (Optional)

Most clients continue working with me after the initial build:
- Monthly review of automation performance
- New workflow builds as your business grows
- Updates when external APIs or tools change
- Priority Slack/WhatsApp support for urgent issues

This runs as a monthly retainer typically between ₹8,000–₹15,000 depending on complexity.
