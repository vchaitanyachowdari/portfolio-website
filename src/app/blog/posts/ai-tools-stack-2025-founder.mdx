## The Danger of Too Many Tools

Every founder I know has a "tool graveyard" — subscriptions that sounded great but never got used, integrations that half-worked, platforms that promised automation but delivered complexity.

I've been there. At one point I had 23 active SaaS subscriptions for a 2-person operation.

The shift I made was toward a **minimal, deeply integrated stack** — fewer tools used more fully. Here's what I've landed on.

---

## The Core Stack (What I Use Every Day)

### Automation Backbone: n8n (Self-Hosted)
**Cost:** ~₹2,000/month (server)  
**What I use it for:** Everything AI-automated in my business. Research agents, lead enrichment, content pipelines, client reporting, email triage. n8n is the central nervous system.

**Why n8n over Make.com or Zapier:** Data sovereignty (client data stays on my server), no per-task pricing, excellent AI node support, and it handles complex multi-branch workflows that Make.com struggles with.

---

### AI Brain: OpenAI GPT-4o + Claude 3.5 Sonnet
**Cost:** ₹3,000–₹6,000/month depending on volume  
**What I use it for:**
- GPT-4o: reasoning, complex analysis, code generation
- Claude: writing, editing, long document synthesis

**The honest truth:** I don't have loyalty to one model. I route different tasks to different models based on what each does better. GPT-4o for structured data. Claude for prose.

---

### Web Research: Serper.dev
**Cost:** ~₹1,500/month  
**What I use it for:** Real-time web search inside n8n workflows. Any time an AI agent needs to know what's happening right now, it searches via Serper.

---

### Content Extraction: Firecrawl
**Cost:** ~₹1,200/month  
**What I use it for:** Fetching, cleaning, and structuring web page content for AI processing. Essential for competitor research and content analysis workflows.

---

### Project Management + Knowledge Base: Notion
**Cost:** Free tier (personal use)  
**What I use it for:** Every deliverable lands in Notion. Client research briefs, project documentation, content calendar, internal wikis. My n8n automations push most content here automatically.

---

### Client Communication: Gmail + Cal.com
**Cost:** Google Workspace: ₹250/month, Cal.com: Free  
**What I use it for:** Gmail is deeply integrated with n8n for email triage and drafting. Cal.com for discovery call scheduling — no back-and-forth email chains.

---

### CRM: HubSpot Free
**Cost:** Free  
**What I use it for:** Tracking deals, contacts, and communication history. n8n pushes new leads directly into HubSpot automatically.

---

### Content Scheduling: Postiz (Self-Hosted)
**Cost:** ~₹800/month (server)  
**What I use it for:** Scheduling and publishing content to LinkedIn, X, and Instagram. AI-generated content is pushed here from my content pipeline, I review and schedule.

---

## The Tools I Tried and Dropped

**Zapier** — Too expensive at scale. ₹8,000+/month for the volume I needed. n8n replaced it completely.

**Jasper.ai** — Sounded great in marketing, mediocre in practice. Direct OpenAI/Claude API calls produce better outputs with more control.

**Notion AI** — The native Notion AI add-on is too generic. My custom prompts via API produce 3x better output.

**Competitor research tools (Semrush, Ahrefs)** — Useful for SEO specifically but expensive for the value I get. n8n + Serper.dev handles 80% of my research needs at 20% of the cost.
