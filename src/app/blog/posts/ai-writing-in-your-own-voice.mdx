## The Problem with "AI-Written" Content

You've seen it. LinkedIn posts that sound like they were written by a committee. Articles that start with "In today's rapidly evolving landscape." Newsletters that say everything and mean nothing.

This is what happens when people outsource their thinking to AI instead of using AI to amplify their thinking.

The goal is not AI-written content. The goal is AI-accelerated content that sounds unmistakably like you.

---

## The Golden Rule: You Bring the Thinking. AI Brings the Production.

Your competitive advantage as a writer is your perspective, your experience, your specific examples, your counterintuitive takes. AI doesn't have any of that.

What AI has is:
- Infinite patience for first drafts
- Ability to restructure and reformat instantly
- No creative block
- Speed

The right model: **You think → AI drafts → You refine**

Not: AI thinks → you review

## 5 AI Writing Workflows That Preserve Your Voice

### Workflow 1: The Voice Memo → Draft

**Best for:** Long-form content (blog posts, newsletters)

1. Record yourself explaining the core idea for 5-10 minutes. Talk like you're explaining to a friend — not writing. Use Otter.ai or your phone's voice memo.
2. Transcribe automatically (Otter, Rev, or Whisper)
3. Paste transcript into ChatGPT/Claude with the prompt:
   > "This is a voice memo transcript of someone explaining an idea. Turn it into a structured blog post in a direct, conversational style. Retain the speaker's specific phrases and examples. Do not add generic filler. Preserve all opinions."
4. You get a rough draft that sounds like you — because it is you.
5. Edit for flow, add examples, refine the ending. 45 minutes total.

---

### Workflow 2: The Idea → LinkedIn Post

**Best for:** Short-form social content

1. Write 2-3 bullet points summarizing your idea (not full sentences, just bullets)
2. Add the prompt:
   > "Turn these notes into a LinkedIn post. Use a strong, specific opening line that isn't a generic question. Write in a direct, first-person style. Use short paragraphs. Max 250 words. End with a question or a call to action. Do NOT use phrases like 'in today's world' or 'it's important to.'"
3. AI generates the draft
4. Replace the opening line with one you actually write (the first line is the most important — make it yours)
5. Edit any phrases that don't sound like you

Total time: 10-15 minutes vs. 45-60 minutes without AI

---

### Workflow 3: Build Your Style Guide First

Before using AI for content, spend 30 minutes creating your personal style guide:

```
My name is [Name]. I write in a [direct/warm/analytical] style.
My audience is [founders/marketers/developers].
I use short paragraphs (max 2-3 sentences).
I never use: "leverage," "synergy," phrases like "in today's world"
I always: Start with a specific observation, Use real examples from my experience
Example of my writing I love: [paste 2-3 examples of your best writing]
```

Give this style guide to Claude at the start of every writing session. The improvement in output quality is dramatic.

---

### Workflow 4: The Contrarian Framework

**Best for:** Opinion posts that get engagement

Instead of using AI to write generic takes, use it to stress-test your contrarian opinions:
1. Write your contrarian take in one sentence
2. Ask AI: "What are the strongest objections to this view?"
3. Ask AI: "What data or examples would support this view?"
4. Write the post yourself, incorporating AI-researched evidence

You end up with an opinion that is yours — backed by evidence you didn't have to manually research.

---

### Workflow 5: Repurposing Engine

**One piece of content → 6 formats:**

From one longform article, generate with AI:
- 3 LinkedIn post angles (different audiences, different angles)
- Twitter/X thread (with specific tweet count and format instructions)
- Email newsletter section
- Instagram caption (visual-first, shorter)
- A conversational podcast script segment

Total repurposing time: 30-40 minutes for 6 assets.

## How to Train AI on Your Voice Over Time

The more examples of your writing you give AI, the better it understands your voice. Maintain a **"My Voice" file** — a Google Doc of your best posts, articles, and emails. Include it (or excerpts) in every major AI writing session.

Over time, AI's approximation of your voice becomes remarkably accurate.

---

## What AI Writing Can't Replace

- **Your specific experiences**: AI can't describe what it was like to lose your first client or close your first deal. You can.
- **Your network knowledge**: "My friend who runs a logistics company told me..." is gold.
- **Your genuine opinion**: The moment readers sense you're not behind the words, you lose them.

AI accelerates. Your authenticity converts.
